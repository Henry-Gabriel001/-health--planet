THE IMMUNE SYSTEM


The immune system is a complex network of organs, cells, and proteins that defends the body against infections, whilst protecting the body's own cell.
The immune system keeps the body a record of every germs (microbe) it has ever defeated so it can recognise and destroy the micrebe quickly if it enter the body again.

WHAT ARE THE THREE TYPES OF IMMUNE SYSTEM?

This protection is called immunity:
Humans have three types of immunity.
1.innate.
2.adaptive.
3.passive.
The main job of the innate immune system is to fight harmful substance and germs that enter the body, for instance through the skin or digestive system.
The adaptive (specific)immune system make antibodies and uses them to specially fight certain germs that the body has previously come in contact with.
